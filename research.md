My research is on various aspects of the interaction between representation theory, quantum field theory, algebraic geometry and topology. 

A central theme is the construction of symplectic singularities and their quantizations from supersymmetric gauge theories in three dimensions.  Most notably, these theories exhibit **3-dimensional mirror symmetry**, a duality that (in particular) exchanges the Higgs branch of one theory with the Coulomb branch of its mirror.  You can learn more about this in my [recent survey article](https://www.ams.org/notices/202309/rnoti-p1395.pdf).

On the mathematical side, I study the geometry and algebra of these spaces—especially Coulomb branches as introduced by Braverman-Finkelberg-Nakajima—and their representation theory.  This includes connections to Cherednik algebras, quantum groups, and various flavors of categorification.
